In-game manual

Player-voiced articles that ship with the game. What the systems are — not the design rationale behind them.

Flight

  • NavigationSwitch between a close flight view and a free, zoomable navigation chart.
  • ScannersYour scanner reports nearby vessels and stations within a limited live-intel bubble.
  • Ships & Hull ClassesRead a contact's built purpose from its hull class and its current actions from doctrine.
  • Pilots & BehaviourA ship's doctrine tells you what its pilot does, independently of the hull class.

Universe

  • Reading Stars and WorldsRead stellar class, multiplicity, and system features as separate facts on your chart.
  • Starlanes & StargatesPowered stargate pairs connect systems through stable starlanes.
  • FoldingFold drives skip the void between distant points through paired temporary wormholes.
  • Black HolesBlack holes drain your speed inside their gravity wells and punish a careless approach.

Economy

  • Cargo & CommoditiesYour cargo hold carries valuable commodities, and everything aboard is lost with your hull.
  • RefiningRefineries turn raw ore into more valuable components before you sell your haul.
  • TradingDock at a buyer to turn the commodities in your hold into credits.
  • Trade DataBuy a trade-data subscription to see prices in systems you're not standing in.
  • IndependentsIndependent prospectors and wreck-pickers mine and salvage for their own account.
  • Your FleetHire hulls at a shipyard and assign each a role — escort, miner, salvager, hauler, or trader.
  • Crew & OfficersHire officers at a shipyard and assign them to your ships — crew is how a hull gets better without a new hull.

Factions

  • TermitusTermitus nest ships consume raw materials and build patient industrial expansion.
  • GorrathThe numerous Gorrath sweep outward, stripping resources before moving to new territory.
  • KitharKithar brokers trade route data, secrets, and overlooked knowledge wherever cargo moves.
  • Verid ConsortiumThe secluded Verid observe Ancient signals with knowledge and power accumulated over centuries.
  • The UnboundThe Unbound turn other ships and wrecks into fused machine-flesh clusters that scan first and never negotiate.
  • Sen'TherSen'Ther hunters fly fast fragile ships and risk their lives on crude fold jumps.

Combat

  • WeaponsWeapon colors now reveal weapon families and variants while bolts and beams demand different responses.
  • Battle StationsSome stations are built to fight — armed emplacements you read by their structure and their flag before you get in range.
  • Ship ModulesGrid-mounted hardware — weapons, engines, and hull tweaks — tested in the arena before it ever reaches a shop.
  • When the Belt Fights BackThe economy fights back. Mined ships call for help, pirate bases hunt them, and militia answer when you bleed, if someone can afford to come.

Other

  • Player StationsBuild your own mining platform in a belt. It costs Alloy Plate and credits, and you get one per system.
  • Credits & LicensesThe artists and creators whose work helps give Vindicator its voice and silhouette.
